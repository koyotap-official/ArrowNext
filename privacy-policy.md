# Arrow Puzzle Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 31, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Arrow Puzzle handles information in the current public build. Arrow Puzzle is a puzzle game that stores gameplay progress and settings on your device.

For privacy questions or support, contact: g1854003@gmail.com

## Summary

- Arrow Puzzle stores progress and settings on your device.
- The current public build does not use live third-party advertising, real-money in-app purchases, external achievement services, analytics SDKs, crash reporting SDKs, cloud save, or account sign-in.
- If those services are added in a future release, this policy and the store privacy disclosures must be updated before that release is published.
- If you contact support by email, your email address and message contents are handled so we can respond to your request.

## Information Stored on Your Device

The app stores local game data so the game can work normally. This may include:

- Current level and unlocked level progress.
- Cleared level records.
- Tutorial and onboarding state.
- Sound and haptic settings.
- Local ad-removal, ad-count, and achievement state used by the game logic.

This data is stored on your device. In the current public build, Arrow Puzzle does not upload this local save data to a server and does not provide cloud synchronization.

Deleting the app or clearing app data may delete this local progress and settings data.

## Information You Send to Support

If you email support, we may receive your email address, the message you send, and any information you choose to include. We use this information to respond to your support request and to understand reported issues.

Do not include sensitive personal information in support emails unless it is necessary for your request.

## Ads, Purchases, Achievements, Analytics, and Crash Reporting

The current public build does not connect live third-party ad networks, real-money purchase processing, platform achievement services, analytics SDKs, or crash reporting SDKs.

Some game systems may have local state for gameplay flow, such as ad counters, ad-removal state, or local achievement state. Those local states do not mean that a live third-party service is active.

If live ads, purchases, platform achievements, analytics, crash reporting, cloud save, account sign-in, or similar services are added later, the policy will be updated to describe the providers, data handled, purposes, retention, sharing, and user choices before the release that enables them.

## Third-Party Services

In the current public build, Arrow Puzzle does not send app data to third-party SDK providers from within the game.

Platform stores, operating systems, and email providers may process information under their own privacy policies when you download the app, manage app permissions, leave a review, or contact support by email.

## Children's Privacy

Arrow Puzzle is not directed to children under 13. The current public build does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through a support email, contact g1854003@gmail.com so the request can be reviewed.

## Data Retention and Deletion

Local progress and settings remain on your device until you delete the app, clear app data, or the app provides a reset flow that removes them.

Support emails are kept only as long as needed for support, record keeping, and issue follow-up. You may contact g1854003@gmail.com to request deletion of support email records associated with your request, subject to any legal or operational need to retain limited records.

Because the current public build does not create user accounts or cloud saves, there is no remote account data to delete from the app.

## Security

We use reasonable measures to protect information handled by the app and support process. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure, and the current build is designed to avoid unnecessary remote data handling.

## Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when the app changes, especially before enabling live ads, real-money purchases, platform achievements, analytics, crash reporting, cloud save, or account sign-in.

The latest version should be posted at the public privacy policy URL used by the app store listing.
